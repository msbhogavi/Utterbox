Read naturally, not at the center of the screen.

Utterbox keeps your script close to the camera line on a MacBook notch or near an external webcam, so eye contact stays believable during demos, calls, and recorded walkthroughs.

Download for macOS View on GitHub
  • Sticky notch mode for MacBook presentations
  • Floating mode for external displays and off-center webcams
  • Voice follow, manual scroll, live resizing, and keyboard control

Two camera placements

Use the notch when the MacBook camera is your eyeline. Switch to a floating prompter when the real webcam lives somewhere else.

Presenter-friendly controls

Resize the overlay in place, scrub with the mouse while playing, and control show or hide, playback, editing, and speed through shortcuts.

Privacy-first defaults

Voice recognition runs on-device by default, cloud fallback is explicit, and script persistence can stay off unless you want drafts remembered.

Built for real speaking setups

The point is not just scrolling text. It is preserving eye contact when your camera, display, and speaking rhythm do not naturally line up.
